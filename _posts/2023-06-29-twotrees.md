A poetic reflection on duality, pairing, and the spaces between—two trees standing in relation, defining each other through proximity and difference.

On a hillside path, two trees grow close enough to share root systems yet far enough apart to maintain distinct identities. Over years of walking past them, I've noticed how their relationship shifts with seasons—sometimes mirroring each other's gestures, sometimes seeming to turn away.

The piece emerged from thinking about pairs: stereo channels, binary oppositions, magnetic poles. How twoness creates a tension that singular things lack. How the space between becomes as significant as the things themselves.
